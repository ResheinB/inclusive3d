Chapter 01 // The Problem

Bridging the Gap
in Visual Perception.

"Design is only truly successful when it is accessible to everyone. This tool turns empathy into an actionable engineering process."

Color Vision Deficiency (CVD) affects approximately 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women globally. In digital design and e-commerce—particularly in interior design and furniture visualization—color communication is critical. Users with CVD often struggle to accurately perceive material colors, leading to mismatched aesthetics and a frustrating user experience.

This final year project bridges the gap by integrating real-time CVD simulation directly into a 3D product viewer. Built with React and Three.js, the application allows users to experience digital furniture models through various simulated color profiles. Designers can ensure their color palettes are accessible, and end-users can make informed decisions regardless of their visual capabilities.
