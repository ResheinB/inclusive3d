Understanding Colour Vision Deficiency

CVD affects approximately 8% of men and 0.5% of women globally. In architecture and interior design, failing to account for CVD creates environments that are confusing, inaccessible, or potentially hazardous.

Protanopia

Reduced sensitivity to red light. Reds, oranges, and yellows appear greener, and colors lose brightness. High contrast patterns are essential to define edges.

Deuteranopia

Reduced sensitivity to green light. The most common form of "red-green" color blindness. Relies heavily on geometric distinctiveness over hue.

Tritanopia

Reduced sensitivity to blue light. Less common, but causes confusion between blues/greens and yellows/violets. Requires rigid texture matrices.

Why Standard Tools Fail

Traditional modeling software requires manual guesswork. Our plugin automates WCAG physical accessibility compliance by applying proprietary non-color cues dynamically.
